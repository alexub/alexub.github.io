I'm a principal ML research scientist at Genesis Therapeutics, working to accelerate drug discovery by combining tools like deep learning, active learning/Bayesian optimization, generative models, and molecular dynamics simulations.

I'm broadly interested in machine learning and its applications to science and engineering. As well as doing math and building software, I like thinking about how to integrate ML with domain-specific techniques, knowledge and workflows to best solve real-world problems.

I did my PhD at Princeton University, advised by Ryan P. Adams, working on accelerating both ML and engineering modeling by developing cheap gradient estimators and learned surrogate models for numerical simulators. I spent two fun summers at Google: at Google Brain in Zurich, and with the speech recognition team in New York. In 2020 I co-organized a NeurIPS workshop on machine learning for engineering: ML4Eng. I come from New Zealand and did my B.Eng at the University of Canterbury, where I worked on signal processing for ventilators in the intensive care unit.

Outside of work, I like films, books, hiking, and traveling.
